How to Publish an App on the Google Play Store

A comprehensive step-by-step guide to successfully launch your Android application on the world's largest app marketplace

1 Prepare Your App for Release
Required

Before uploading to Play Console, make sure your app is properly prepared:

  • Use a release build: Build your APK/AAB in release mode. Use Android App Bundle (.aab) — Google requires AAB for new apps.
  • Sign your app: You must sign the app with a release keystore. In Android Studio: Build > Generate Signed Bundle / APK
  • Test thoroughly: Make sure there are no crashes, no logging of sensitive data, and performance is optimized.

Note: Android App Bundle is the preferred publishing format. It allows Google Play to generate optimized APKs for each user's device configuration, resulting in smaller download sizes.

2 Create a Google Play Developer Account
One-time

Go to: https://play.google.com/console

You will need:

  • A Google account
  • A one-time $25 USD registration fee
  • Accept the Developer Distribution Agreement

Once approved, you can access Play Console.

3 Create a New Application
Required

In the Play Console:

  • Go to All Apps
  • Click Create App
  • Choose:
    • App name
    • Default language
    • App type: App or Game
    • Free or Paid
  • Declare that the app meets policies
  • Click Create App
4 Complete Store Listing
Required

You must fill out:

  • App Details: App Name, Short Description, Full Description
  • Graphics: Upload:
    • App icon (512×512)
    • Feature graphic (1,024×500)
    • Screenshots (phone/tablet, JPG or PNG)
    • Optional promo video (YouTube URL)
  • Categorization: Category (e.g., Tools, Education, Games), Tags, Content rating questionnaire
  • Contact information: Email (required), Website (optional), Phone number (optional)
5 App Content Requirements
Required

Under App content, complete:

  • Privacy policy (must be a public URL)
  • Ads declaration (whether your app uses ads)
  • Target audience & age rating
  • Data safety form
  • Permissions usage (especially sensitive ones)
  • Content rating questionnaire

Important: The Data Safety section is required for all apps. You must accurately declare how your app collects, shares, and handles user data.

6 Upload the App Bundle (.aab)
Required
  • Go to Production → Releases
  • Click Create new release
  • Upload your .aab file
  • Add release notes (e.g., "Initial release")
7 Start Pre-Launch Testing (Optional but Recommended)
Recommended

Google allows different testing tracks:

  • Internal testing: Up to 100 testers
  • Closed testing: Select groups
  • Open testing: Anyone with link

This helps catch crashes before public release.

8 Review Policy and Pricing Setup
Required
  • Set pricing (free/paid)
  • Set country availability
  • Ensure no violations of:
    • User Data policy
    • Safety policies
    • Device & Network abuse
    • Content restrictions
9 Publish the App
Final Step

Once everything is complete:

  • Go to Production → Releases
  • Click Review Release
  • Fix any warnings
  • Click Start rollout to Production
  • Confirm
10 Google Review Process
Waiting Period

After submission:

  • Review typically takes hours to a few days
  • First-time developers may take longer (24–7 days)
  • You will get an email notification when approved or if changes are required
11 After Publishing
Monitoring

You can monitor:

  • App statistics
  • Crashes (via Play Console or Firebase Crashlytics)
  • User reviews
  • Android Vitals
  • Store performance metrics

You can release updates anytime by uploading a new version.
